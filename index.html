Steve Job

"I want to put a ding in the universe."

General Information:

The one who will always be one of the most influencial name in the technology industry, the CEO, co-founder of Apple,Inc. The creator of many wonderful life changing devices that really changed the world overnight.

The timeline of his life story is as follow:
  • February 24th, 1955: Born in San Francisco but was left by his biological parents and put up for adoption, later adopted by the Jobs family
  • 1961: Move to Moutain View, California.
  • 1968: Got in contact with Bill Hewlett (the co-founder of Hewlett-Packard) and was offered an internship
  • 1970: Met up with Steve Wozniak, who would later become the co-founder of Apple,Inc. alongside Jobs. According to a biography of Wozniak, the two hit it off immediately despite the four-year age gap.
  • 1972: Graduate from Homestead High School, Cupertino. Went on to attend Reeds College, drop out only a semester later after attended only the class he liked.
  • 1974: Working nightshift as a engineer at Atari, tried to create a single-player Pong game, which later became the game called Breakout.
  • 1976: Co-founded Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, rolled out the Apple I as a kit for $666.66 that same year.
  • January 3rd, 1977: Founded Apple,Inc.
  • June 5th, 1977: Rolled out the Apple II, the very first commercial personal computer with a case and colored graphics. It became Apple's first successful personal computer product.
  • December 12th, 1980: Apple,Inc. went public, Jobs's net worth rose to $200 million.
  • January 24th, 1984: The Macintosh was born, the very first small personal computer with a graphical user interface, introduced in a $1.5 million Super Bowl commercial. The voice of the computer that became the slogan was "Never trust a computer you can't lift."
  • September 12th, 1985: At the age of 30, fired from Apple,Inc. by John Sculley, founded NeXT Computer (later became NeXT Software). A NeXT workstation was used by CERN as a server for the World Wide Web.
  • February 3rd, 1986: Bought the graphics division of LucasFilm which becomes Pixar Animation Studio for $10 million.
  • 1988: NeXT Computer released its first computer.
  • 1993: Discontinued the hardware department at NeXT Computer, focus more on software and later changed its name to NeXT Software
  • November 29th, 1995: Became Pixar's CEO. The studio released the movie Toy Story with celebrity Tom Hanks voicing the character, the movie was a very big hit.
  • December 10th, 1996: Apple,Inc. bought NeXT Software for $429 million, went back to Apple as an advisor.
  • July 9th, 1997: Became CEO of Apple,Inc. first as de facto chief, then as nterim chief in September that same year.
  • August 6th, 1997: Microsoft invest $150 million and coupled with the partnership for Microsoft Office and Internet Explorer to be available for the Mac.
  • November 10th, 1997: Introduced Apple Store where people can buy customed-order Apple products that would be delivered to the home.
  • January 8th, 1998: Apple,Inc. returned to its profitable state.
  • May 6th, 1998: Introduced the original iMac, available in August that same year.
  • January 5th, 2000: Drop the "interim" from his CEO title, take a $1 annual salary. Terminated projects such as OpenDoc, Newton. Brought NeXT Software products into Apple,Inc., that laid the foundation for the later Mac OS.
  • January 9th, 2001: Introduced iTunes exclusively for Mac users. "iTunes is miles ahead of every other jukebox application, and we hope its dramatically simpler user interface will bring even more people into the digital music revolution."
  • March 24th, 2001: Shipped the very first version of the Mac OS, code name "Cheetah"
  • October 23rd, 2001: Introduced the very first iPod. "With iPod, Apple has invented a whole new category of digital music player that lets you put your entire music collection in your pocket and listen to it wherever you go...With iPod, listening to music will never be the same again."
  • July 17th, 2002: The first Windows-compatible iPods were introduced.
  • January 11th, 2005: Introduced the iPod Shuffle. "iPod Shuffle is smaller and lighter than a pack of gum and costs less than $100...With most flash memory music players, users must use tiny displays and complicated controls to find their music; with iPod Shuffle you just relax, and it serves up new combinations of your music every time you listen."
  • June 6th, 2005: Announced the switch from IBM to Intel chip for the newly introduced Macbook Pro and iMac
  • September 7th, 2005: Introduced the iPod Nano. "iPod Nano is the biggest revolution since the original iPod...iPod Nano is a full-featured iPod in an impossibly small size, and it's going to change the rules for the entire portable music market."
  • October 12th, 2005: Introduced the iPod Video. "This is the best music player we've ever made...It's 30 percent thinner and has 50 percent more capacity for the same price, and you get video as a bonus."
  • January 25th, 2006: Disney bought Pixar for $7.4 billion. He joined the board as its top shareholder.
  • January 9th, 2007: Completely drop the "Computer" for the company name, introduced the first iPhone. "iPhone is a revolutionary and magical product that is literally five years ahead of any other mobile phone...We are all born with the ultimate pointing device--our fingers--and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."
  • September 5th, 2007: Introduced the iPod Touch. "The iPod Touch is a landmark iPod, ushering in a whole new generation of features based on its revolutionary multitouch interface and built-in Wi-Fi wireless networking...People are going to be amazed at how thin it is and how much it does."
  • December 5th, 2007: Inducted in the hall of fame of California by Gov. Arnold Schwartzenegger.
  • January 15th, 2008: Released App Store as an update to the existing iTunes, also the Macbook Air was released.
  • January 27th, 2010: Introduced the iPad.
  • March 2nd, 2011: After 3 medical leaves for treatment and transplant, he made a surprise appearance to wrap up the keynote of the new iPad 2
  • June 6th, 2011: Appear at WWDC for the last time.
  • August 24th, 2011: Resign from Apple,Inc. as CEO.
  • October 5th, 2011: Died from pancreatic cancer.
